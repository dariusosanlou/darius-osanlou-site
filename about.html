About

Meet Darius.

Body psychologist, online coach, and someone who believes we're capable of far more than we often give ourselves credit for.

I've spent most of my adult life trying to understand why people struggle to become the person they want to be.

That curiosity led me into psychology, mental health, medical research, and physique development. I completed a Master's degree in Clinical Affective Disorders at King's College London, worked in the NHS across various psychological roles for four years, spent years interpreting clinical research as a medical writer, and have trained consistently for over 17 years, winning a Classic Bodybuilding competition in 2022.

The NHS work shaped how I coach more than anything else on my CV. I kept seeing the same pattern: people coming in with anxiety and depression, and when you took a proper history of their daily life, their lifestyle was often making things significantly worse. Poor sleep, skipping meals, caffeine on an empty stomach, no structure, no movement. Not the root cause of everything, but fuel on the fire. Learning to sit with someone, build a complete picture of their life, and identify what they couldn't see themselves was some of the most formative work I've done. Four years and hundreds of patients from all backgrounds taught me how to ask the right questions and, more importantly, how to hear what someone isn't saying. Most coaches can't do that.

The bodybuilding taught me something different.

I went into competing wanting to prove something to myself, and in some ways I did. I discovered a level of dedication and endurance I hadn't known was there, learned to use setbacks as fuel, and willingly went to extremes most people wouldn't consider. I won. And when my name was called out, I felt relief. Not joy. Relief.

That told me everything. I'd put my entire sense of worth on the outcome, and winning didn't deliver what I thought it would. The goalposts just moved. That experience changed how I think about goals, progress, and what people are really chasing when they try to change their bodies. It's woven into how I work with every client on that side of things.

But the reason I coach goes deeper than qualifications or competition.

In 2023, my best friend Liam passed away unexpectedly at just 26 years old.

We trained together for over a decade, and had been friends since the age of three. He was one of the people who had the greatest impact on my life.

His death made me question how I'd been living. Playing it safe, avoiding risk, justifying a careful existence to myself when I knew I was capable of more. I no longer plan on arriving safely at death. I plan on living fully, which means tackling fears, taking up space, and having a real impact on the people I work with.

Body Psychologist was my answer.

Today, I help people improve their physique, health, confidence, and quality of life through evidence-based coaching that combines psychology, training, nutrition, and behaviour change.

Because changing your body is rarely just about your body.

It's about the life you get to live because of it.

My Approach

What you can expect.

Understand Yourself

Most people know what to do. The gap is understanding why they keep not doing it. Four years in the NHS taught me to find the patterns, blind spots and daily habits that nobody else has thought to ask about.

Process Over Outcome

I competed and won. I also experienced firsthand what happens when you hinge your sense of worth on an outcome. The work is about building a real relationship with the process, not just a finish line to collapse across.

Human First

Your physique matters. So does your sleep, your stress load, your confidence and your quality of life. Coaching that ignores the rest of the picture doesn't hold up when real life gets in the way.
