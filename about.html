About

Meet Darius.

I didn't get into coaching because I had an easy relationship with my body. I got into it because I didn't.

With time I realised that people often struggle not for their lack of effort, but because nobody has helped them understand why they do what they do in the first place.

I have an MSc in Affective Disorders from King's College London and a BSc in Psychology from Nottingham Trent University. Before becoming a coach, I worked in medical research, studying the relationship between the brain, body, and behaviour. What I found, consistently, is that physical transformation is rarely just a physical problem.

My clients are often disciplined, high-functioning people who keep hitting the same walls. Some are managing health conditions that make standard advice useless. Others have a complicated relationship with food that no macro calculator will fix. Their careers, stress, and home lives shape their bodies just as surely as anything they eat or lift.

My job is to identify what's getting in your way and build around it. Not with a template or someone else's programme, but with an approach that accounts for your psychology, physiology, history, and life.

That's what Body Psychology means in practice.
