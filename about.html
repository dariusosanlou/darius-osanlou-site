About

Meet Darius.

I didn't get into coaching because I had an easy relationship with my body. I got into it because I didn't, and because the more I studied the science of human behaviour, the more I realised that people often fail not for a lack of effort, but because nobody has helped them understand why they do what they do.

I have an MSc in Affective Disorders from King's College London and a BSc in Psychology from Nottingham Trent University. Before I became a coach, I worked in medical research. I've spent years studying the relationship between the brain, the body, stress, and behaviour, and what I found consistently is that physical transformation is rarely a physical problem.

The clients I work with tend to reflect that. They're not beginners. They're often people who are disciplined and high-functioning, but who keep hitting the same walls. People managing health conditions that make standard advice useless. People whose relationship with food has become complicated in ways that a macro calculator will never fix. People whose careers, stress levels, and home lives are doing as much to shape their body as anything they eat or lift.

My job is to work out exactly what's getting in the way for you, and build something around that. Not a template. Not a programme designed for someone else. Something that accounts for your psychology, your physiology, your history, and your life.

That's what Body Psychology means in practice.
