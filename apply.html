Before You Apply

Common questions.

How long until I notice a difference?

Most people feel a shift within the first two to three weeks: less noise around food, more consistency with training. Visible physical change typically starts from weeks four to six. Bigger transformations take months, but the feeling that something has genuinely changed tends to come early.

What if I travel a lot or my schedule is unpredictable?

That's exactly the kind of thing we build around. Most clients aren't people with simple, structured lives. They're busy people with unpredictable weeks, work travel, and social lives that don't pause. The whole point is building something that works in your actual life, not the version where everything goes smoothly.

Do I have to count calories or cut out foods I enjoy?

The nutrition approach is built on understanding your relationship with food and making sustainable changes, not obsessing over numbers. Some people find tracking useful at certain points; others don't. We work out what suits you.

Can I still drink alcohol?

Yes. We'll be honest about how it affects the body and your sleep, and we'll factor it into how we approach nutrition. But the goal isn't to strip everything enjoyable from your life. That's not a sustainable way to live, and it's not how I coach.

I've tried coaching before and it didn't stick. What's different?

Most coaching stops at the plan: what to eat, how to train. What's usually missing is the work underneath: the stress responses, the identity patterns, the habits that keep pulling things back. That's the part I focus on. The plan is necessary, but it's rarely the reason things don't stick.

What if I'm not completely sure yet?

The application isn't a commitment. It's a conversation. If you're on the fence, applying is the right move. It costs two minutes and gives you the information you need to make a proper decision. If it turns out it's not the right time, that's fine. There's no pressure.
