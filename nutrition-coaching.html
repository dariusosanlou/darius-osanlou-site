Nutrition Coaching

The Approach

Gut, brain and behaviour.

For my MSc in Clinical Affective Disorders at King's College London, my systematic review focused on the gut-brain axis, specifically how the microbiome influences mood, cognition, motivation and stress response.

What that research made clear is something most nutrition coaching ignores entirely: what you eat doesn't just affect your body composition. It affects how you feel, how you think, and how much mental capacity you have to make good decisions. The relationship runs in both directions.

That's why nutrition coaching with me is never just about macros. We look at the gut-brain connection, the role of stress hormones in fat storage and cravings, and the psychological patterns like restriction cycles, emotional eating and all-or-nothing thinking that make nutrition feel harder than it should.

Food is information. It signals to your gut microbiome, your hormones, your mood and your energy. Get it right for your body and your life, and everything else becomes easier.

Who This Is For

Who nutrition coaching works for.

This is for you if

  • You've tried tracking calories, cutting carbs or following plans and keep ending up back at square one
  • Stress, emotions or habit keep pulling you toward food in ways you know aren't serving you
  • You want to understand nutrition rather than just follow rules someone else made up
  • You have a complex health condition: coeliac, endometriosis, type 1 diabetes, RED-S, or a history of disordered eating
  • You're already training consistently and want the nutrition side to actually match the effort you're putting in

This is not for you if

  • You want a generic meal plan you could have found for free online
  • You're looking for a crash diet or a 6-week shred
  • You want to be told exactly what to eat without understanding why
  • You're not willing to look honestly at your patterns

Progress

How we measure results.

Energy and mood

How you feel throughout the day is often the earliest signal that nutrition is working. Better energy and more stable mood usually show up before the scales move.

Digestion and gut health

Bloating, irregular digestion, and low energy after meals are not just annoying. They're informative. We track these and respond to them.

Body composition

Measured through how you look, feel and perform, rather than a single number that fluctuates for reasons entirely unrelated to fat loss.

Progress photos are available as an optional tracking tool if that's useful for you. They're never a requirement, and they're never shared without your explicit consent.

Complex Needs

When your situation is more complicated.

A significant number of my clients come with health conditions that make standard nutrition advice useless, or actively harmful. I've worked with people managing:

Coeliac disease and intolerances

Nutrition planning that respects real dietary constraints, not just swapping out bread and hoping for the best.

RED-S and hormonal disruption

Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport requires a careful, progressive approach to fuelling. Under-eating your way to a better physique makes this worse, not better.

Type 1 diabetes and endometriosis

Conditions that interact directly with nutrition require an approach built around your actual physiology, not a generic template.

I'm not a dietitian or a medical professional. If you're managing a complex condition, I work alongside your existing medical care, not as a replacement for it. But I do know how to build a nutrition approach that works in the real world of living with these conditions.
